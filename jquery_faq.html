Frequently Asked Questions

1. Who is the director of the National Parks Service?
Jonathan B. Jarvis
2. What government agency oversees the National Park Service?
The U.S. Department of the Interior
3. How many employees are in the National Park Service
22,000 employees and 221,000 volunteers
4. How old is the National Park System?
It was created on August 25, 1916.
5. What is the origin of the National Park Service arrowhead?
The arrowhead represents historical and archaeological values.
6. What is the largest NPS site?
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Reserve in AK with 13.2 million acres
7. What is the smallest?
Thaddeus Koscuiszko National Memorial in PA with 0.02 acres
8. How many people visit national parks?
In 2014, over 290 million people.
9. How many areas are there in the NPS system?
There are 407 areas covering over 84 million acres accross each state and several territories.
10. Which park was the first in the NPS system?
Yellowstone National Park was established by an act signed by President Grant on March 1, 1872.
